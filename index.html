Active Transport

  • Uses membrane proteins to transport molecules across cells;
  • Is energy driven, meaning that it gets its energy from cell respiration, usually in form of ATP;
  • Active transport is necessary, because it moves substances up the concentration gradient.
Active Transport is a energy-driven process, usually using ATP, where membrane proteins transport molecules across cells, mainly classified as primary or secondary, based on how energy is coupled to fuel these mechanisms. This is done through the use of energy produced by cell respiration. The substances move from a low concentrated area to a high concentrated area, which is why active transport if necessary in the first place. If a substance has a higher concentration inside the cell, then the substance would defuse out of the cell, making active transport redundant.

Active Transport

  • Usually used to accumulate needed molecules like glucose and amino acids;
  • In humans, active transport happens massively in the small intestine;
  • In plants, it largely occurs in the root hair cells and phloem.
Specifically, active transport is usually used by cells to accumulate needed molecules such as glucose and amino acids. In mamals like humans, a hot-spots for active transport is, for example, the small intestine. However, as plants have no small intestine, active transport largely occurs in root-hair cells and phloem for them, where minerals and sucrose are transported respectively.

Diffusion

Osmosis

  • Diffusion of water through a permeable membrane;
  • So a situation where the water molecules can pass through a membrane,
  • But the solute molecules, like salt and minerals, can’t.
Osmosis is really just a type of diffusion; specifically, it’s the diffusion of water molecules in a situation where these molecules, but not solute molecules like salt and minerals, can pass through a membrane.

Osmosis

  • Water concentration is measured in water potential:
  • A dilute solution (more water) as a high water potential,
  • A concentrated solution (less water) has a low water potential.

Osmosis

  • Aninmal cells are partially permeable, so osmosis happens;
  • But this can be dangerous — as animal cells have low water potential — and can cause the cell to burst.
  • Plant cells don't suffer from this, as they have a cell wall.
  • However, the membrane can still detach from the cell wall, which is called plasmolysation.

Planned Studies

Study groups can offer a more fun way of revision, as you are doing it together with friends and classmates, thus giving you more motivation to study.

Study Groups

With having a planned out revision schedule, it is easier and more effective to study as you know what is expected from you and what you should aim for. Each subject will also be organized neatly in a checklist and will be tiched of once completed.

Revision Techniques

We think it is better to use planned studies for two reasons. Firstly, while a study group can be fun, it can also be distracting as learning and revising together with friends can quickly lead to things like talking, playing games, etc. Secondly, having planned studies can be more directive. With knowing what you are about to learn, your mind becomes focused on it, leading to more effective revision unlike study groups which just learn topics blindly.
